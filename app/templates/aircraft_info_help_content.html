Registration: The aircraft’s unique registration number (tail number).

Aircraft Type: The general model of the aircraft (e.g., Boeing 737).

Airline: The airline or operator associated with the aircraft.

Country of Registration: The country where the aircraft is officially registered.

Year of Manufacture: The year the aircraft was built.

Aircraft Age: The age of the aircraft calculated from its manufacture year.

Times Seen: The number of times this aircraft has been recorded by the system.

First Seen: The date and time this aircraft was first spotted by the system.

Last Sighted: The most recent sighting of this aircraft.

Category: Type of service (e.g., passenger, cargo, military).

Manufacturer: The company that built the aircraft.

Model: Specific aircraft model (e.g., 737-800).

Serial Number (MSN): Manufacturer Serial Number of the aircraft.

Maximum Takeoff Weight: The highest weight the aircraft can safely take off at, in kilograms.

Engine Count: Number of engines installed on the aircraft.

Engine Manufacturer: Company that built the engines.

Engine Type: Type of engine used (e.g., jet, turboprop).

Engine Model: Specific engine model used on the aircraft.

Fuel Type: The type of fuel the aircraft runs on.

Registration Type: Indicates how the aircraft is registered (e.g., private, commercial).

Registered Owner: The name of the company (if available) that owns the aircraft.

Owner Location: The city or suburb where the owner is based.

First Registration Date: Date the aircraft was officially registered.

Airframe Type: Indicates the structural configuration of the aircraft.

Propeller Manufacturer: Name of the propeller maker (if applicable).

Type Certification: The approval designation for this aircraft design.

Country of Manufacture: Country where the aircraft was produced.

ICAO Type Designator: ICAO’s standard short aircraft type code.
